CSci 227: Programming Methods
Department of Computer Science
Hunter College, City University of New York
Spring 2024

TL;DR: Data structures & algorithms in Python for CS Minors.

For questions about the course, write to: stjohn AT hunter cuny edu.

Announcements:

  • This course is designed as a continuation of CSci 127 and aimed at computer science minors and others interested in strengthening their programming skills and knowledge of canonical data structures and algorithms. This course can be counted towards a computer science minor, but not towards the computer science major.
  • If you have taken the introductory programming course at Hunter (CSci 127), you can enroll directly via CUNYFirst without additional permissions. If you took the course elsewhere, reach out to a computer science advisor about what's needed to align your previous courses to Hunter courses on your CUNYFirst record.

Course Structure:

Each we have:
  • In-person Lecture: Tuesday 10:00-11:15am, 1001G HN.
  • Online Lab: asynchronous, schedule below.
  • In-person Quizzes: By appointment in 1001G Hunter North.
  • In-person Code Reviews (oral assessment): By appointment in 1001G Hunter North.
  • Programming Assignments

This is a HYBRID course which means that, in place of a second weekly meeting, you are responsible for independently working through the weekly lab found in the "Handouts" column in the Course Outline below.
