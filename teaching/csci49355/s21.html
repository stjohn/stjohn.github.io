This undergraduate topics course focuses on advanced topics in the design and analysis of algorithms.

The course has lectures on Mondays and Thursday afternoons.

Prerequisites are CSci 265 (Computer Theory I) and CSci 335 (Software Analysis and Design III).

Programming projects are in Python. For a refresher on Python, see:

Combines theoretical analysis with programming. Required course participation, weekly quizzes, 3 programming projects, and final exam.

Possible topics:

  • Sorting-- warm-up to introduce complexity analysis, worst-case, best-case, average-case, recurrence relations (not sure they're covered earlier)
  • Trees-- big part of Stewart's class. Check and see what was done with them-- focus on edit distances? Or visualizations? Suffix trees? Rotations, phylo trees, BST's for floats
  • Is network flow covered?
  • Dynamic Programming-- is this covered in 335? Shortest paths, MSA, and friends
  • Hashes and friends: hashing variants, Bloom filters, ...
  • Probabilistic/Randomized Algorithms: Markov Chains, Hashes, Bloom Filters, etc.
  • Sketches and shingleprinting approaches, algorithms with predictions (a la M Mitzenmacher)
  • Dimensionality Reduction approaches
  • Motivate it as a reading seminar w/ programming. Focus on trees. Can include open questions?
